# United Nations Sustainable Development Goals

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are the blueprint adopted by the United Nations to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all. They address the global challenges we face, including poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace, and justice. There are a total of seventeen SDGs as listed below. The goals **selected** as directly relevant to OBSCURA are marked with a filled box (■); the remainder are listed for completeness.

| | Goal | | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| ☐ | 1. No Poverty | ☐ | 10. Reduced Inequalities |
| ☐ | 2. Zero Hunger | ☐ | 11. Sustainable Cities and Communities |
| ☐ | 3. Good Health and Well-being | ☐ | 12. Responsible Consumption and Production |
| ☐ | 4. Quality Education | ☐ | 13. Climate Action |
| ☐ | 5. Gender Equality | ☐ | 14. Life Below Water |
| ☐ | 6. Clean Water and Sanitation | ☐ | 15. Life on Land |
| ☐ | 7. Affordable and Clean Energy | **■** | **16. Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions** |
| ☐ | 8. Decent Work and Economic Growth | ☐ | 17. Partnerships to Achieve the Goals |
| **■** | **9. Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure** | | |

**SDG 9 — Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure** is supported by OBSCURA through its contribution to resilient digital infrastructure: by automating dark-web monitoring, the platform strengthens an organisation's ability to anticipate and respond to threats against its information systems, advancing the innovation and security posture that SDG 9.c specifically calls for.

**SDG 16 — Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions** is supported through OBSCURA's role in helping legitimate institutions detect cybercrime preparation — leaked credentials, stolen data, malware sales, and pre-attack chatter — earlier than would be possible by manual means. Faster, evidence-backed detection enables more effective response by security operations centres and law-enforcement liaisons, contributing directly to SDG 16.4's call for the reduction of illicit financial and arms flows and the strengthening of recovery and return of stolen assets, and to SDG 16.a's call for the building of relevant national institutions for preventing violence and combating crime.
